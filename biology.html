Biology Units

Unit 1: Diversity in Living World

The Living World: Biodiversity, taxonomy, systematics, species concept, taxonomical hierarchy, binomial nomenclature.

Biological Classification: Five kingdom classification, Monera, Protista, Fungi, Lichens, Viruses and Viroids.

Plant Kingdom: Algae, Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms classification.

Animal Kingdom: Non-chordates and chordates classification.

Unit 2: Structural Organisation in Animals and Plants

Morphology of flowering plants: Root, stem, leaf, flower, fruit, seed and plant families.

Anatomy of flowering plants: Plant tissues, tissue systems, dicot and monocot anatomy.

Animal tissues: Epithelial, Connective, Muscular and Neural tissues. Frog anatomy.

Unit 3: Cell Structure and Function

Cell theory, prokaryotic/eukaryotic cells, organelles, mitochondria, plastids, ribosomes, nucleus.

Biomolecules: Carbohydrates, Lipids, Proteins, Nucleic acids, Enzymes.

Cell cycle, Mitosis, Meiosis and significance.

Unit 4: Plant Physiology

Photosynthesis: Pigments, photochemical phase, Calvin cycle, C3 & C4 pathway, photorespiration.

Respiration: Glycolysis, fermentation, TCA cycle, ETS and respiratory quotient.

Plant growth regulators, photoperiodism and vernalization.

🎥 Visit StudyByPKY YouTube Channel Buy THE SUPER 50 COMPUTER BASED TEST by filing Google form
Unit 5: Human Physiology

Breathing and exchange of gases, respiration mechanism.

Body fluids and circulation: Blood, cardiac cycle, ECG, double circulation.

Excretory system, urine formation, osmoregulation.

Locomotion, muscles, joints and disorders.

Neural control, neuron, CNS and sensory organs.

Chemical coordination: Endocrine glands, hormones and hormone action.

Unit VI: Reproduction Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

Microsporogenesis, Megasporogenesis, Pollination types/agencies, Outbreeding devices, Double Fertilization, Endosperm/Embryo development, Apomixis, and Parthenocarpy.

Human Reproduction: Male and female reproductive systems; Spermatogenesis and Oogenesis; Hormonal regulation of the Menstrual cycle; Fertilisation, implantation, placenta formation, and parturition.

Reproductive Health: Population explosion and birth control methods (contraceptives); Medical Termination of Pregnancy (MTP), STDs, and Infertility treatments (IVF, ZIFT, GIFT).

Unit VII: Genetics and Evolution principles of Inheritance and Variation

: Mendelian inheritance, deviations (Incomplete dominance, Co-dominance, Multiple alleles), Polygenic inheritance, Chromosomal theory, Sex determination, and genetic disorders (Haemophilia, Sickle-cell anemia, Thalassemia).

Molecular Basis of Inheritance: Search for genetic material (Griffith, Hershey-Chase), DNA/RNA structure, DNA Packaging, Replication, Transcription, Genetic Code, Translation, and Gene Regulation (Lac Operon).

Evolution: Origin of life, evidence for evolution (homologous vs. analogous structures), Darwin’s theory, Modern Synthetic theory, Hard-winberg Principle, Adaptive Radiation, and Human Evolution.

Unit VIII: Biology in Human Welfare human Health and Disease

: Pathogens causing Malaria, Typhoid, Pneumonia, Elephantiasis; Innate and Acquired immunity, T and B cells, Antibodies, Vaccines, Allergy, Autoimmunity, AIDS, Cancer, and Drug/Alcohol abuse.

Microbes in Human Welfare: Microbes in household food processing, industrial production (antibiotics, enzymes), sewage treatment (primary and secondary), biogas production, and biocontrol/biofertilizer agents

Unit IX: BiotechnologyBiotechnology - Principles and Processes

: Genetic engineering technology, Restriction enzymes, Cloning vectors (pBR322 details), PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) steps, and Downstream processing.

Biotechnology and its Applications: Genetically Modified Organisms (Bt Cotton, Pest-resistant plants), Insulin production, Gene Therapy, Molecular Diagnosis (ELISA, PCR), and Transgenic animals

Unit X: Ecology and EnvironmentOrganisms and Populations

: Organism-environment relationships, population attributes (birth/death rates, age pyramids), and population interactions (Mutualism, Competition, Predation, Parasitism).

Ecosystem: Ecosystem structure and function; Productivity (Primary, Secondary) and Decomposition; Energy flow (food chains/webs); Ecological Pyramids (number, biomass, energy).

Biodiversity and Its Conservation: Patterns and importance of biodiversity; Loss of biodiversity, causes (Evil Quartet); In-situ conservation (National parks, Sanctuaries, Biosphere reserves) and Ex-situ conservation
