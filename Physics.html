Physics Important Units

Class 11 Detailed Syllabus

Unit I

: Physical World and MeasurementUnits and Measurements: SI units, fundamental and derived units, accuracy,

precision, errors, significant figures, dimensions of physical quantities, and dimensional analysis.

Unit II

; KinematicsMotion in a Straight Line: Frame of reference, position-time graphs, speed, velocity,

uniform/non-uniform motion, and average speed.Motion in a Plane: Vectors, scalar products,

vector resolution, relative velocity, projectile motion, and uniform circular motion.

Unit III

: Laws of MotionForce and Inertia: Newton’s laws of motion, momentum, impulse, law of conservation of linear

momentum, equilibrium of concurrent forces, static and kinetic friction, laws of friction, rolling friction,

and lubrication.Dynamics of Uniform Circular Motion: Centripetal force and examples (vehicles on a level/banked circular road).

Unit IV

: Work, Energy, and PowerWork & Kinetic Energy: Kinetic and potential energy, work-energy theorem, power,

conservative and non-conservative forces, conservation of mechanical energy, and elastic/inelastic collisions.

Unit V

: Motion of System of Particles and Rigid BodyRotational Motion: Center of mass of a two-particle system,

momentum conservation, moment of a force, torque, angular momentum, conservation of angular momentum,

equilibrium of rigid bodies, moment of inertia, radius of gyration, and parallel/perpendicular axes theorems.

Unit VI

: GravitationGravitational Forces: Kepler’s laws of planetary motion, universal law of gravitation,

acceleration due to gravity (g) and its variation with altitude/depth, gravitational potential energy,

escape velocity, and orbital velocity of a satellite.

Unit VII

: Properties of Bulk MatterMechanical Properties of Solids: Elastic behavior, stress-strain relationship,

Hooke's law, Young's modulus, bulk modulus, and shear modulus.Mechanical Properties of Fluids: Pressure due to

a fluid column, Pascal's law, buoyancy, viscosity, Stokes' law, terminal velocity, streamline/turbulent flow,

critical velocity, Bernoulli's theorem, surface energy, and surface tension (angle of contact, drops, bubbles).

Thermal Properties of Matter: Heat, temperature, thermal expansion, specific heat capacity, calorimetry, latent heat,

heat transfer (conduction, convection, radiation), thermal conductivity, and Wien’s displacement law.

Unit VIII

: ThermodynamicsThermal Laws & Engines: Thermal equilibrium, zeroth law, first law (internal energy, work, heat),

isothermal and adiabatic processes, second law of thermodynamics, reversible/irreversible processes, heat engines, and refrigerators.

Unit IX

: Kinetic Theory of GasesGas Dynamics: Equation of state of a perfect gas, work done on compressing a gas, kinetic theory of gases (assumptions, concept of pressure), kinetic energy and temperature, degrees of freedom, law of equipartition of energy, and mean free path.

Unit X

Unit X

: Oscillations and WavesOscillations: Periodic motion, Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM), oscillations of a spring,

kinetic and potential energy in SHM, and a simple pendulum.Waves: Wave motion, longitudinal and transverse waves,

speed of wave motion, displacement relation for a progressive wave, principle of superposition of waves, reflection of waves,

standing waves in strings/organ pipes, fundamental mode, harmonics, and beats.

Class 12 Detailed Syllabus

Unit Xl

: ElectrostaticsElectric Charges & Fields: Coulomb's law, electric field, electric field lines, electric dipole,

electric flux, Gauss’s law and its applications (infinitely long straight wire, uniformly charged infinite plane sheet,

uniformly charged thin spherical shell).Electrostatic Potential & Capacitance: Electric potential, potential difference,

equipotential surfaces, electrical potential energy, conductors, dielectrics and electric polarization, capacitors and capacitance,

parallel plate capacitor (with and without dielectric medium), and energy stored in a capacitor.

Unit XII

: Current ElectricityElectric Currents: Ohm's law, electrical resistance, V-I characteristics, electrical energy and power,

resistivity, conductivity, carbon resistors, temperature dependence of resistance, internal resistance of a cell,

potential difference and emf of a cell, combination of cells (series and parallel), Kirchhoff's laws, Wheatstone bridge, and meter bridge.

Unit XIII

: Magnetic Effects of Current and MagnetismMoving Charges: Biot-Savart law and its application to a current-carrying

circular loop, Ampere's law and its applications (straight wire, solenoid), force on a moving charge in uniform magnetic/electric

fields, cyclotron, force on a current-carrying conductor in a uniform magnetic field, force between two parallel current-carrying

conductors, torque on a current loop, and moving coil galvanometer.Magnetism: Current loop as a magnetic dipole, magnetic dipole moment,

bar magnet as an equivalent solenoid, magnetic field lines, Earth's magnetic field, magnetic properties of materials (paramagnetic,

diamagnetic, ferromagnetic), electromagnets, and permanent magnets.

Unit XIV

: Electromagnetic Induction and Alternating CurrentsEMI: Electromagnetic induction, Faraday's laws, induced EMF and current,

Lenz's Law, eddy currents, and self/mutual induction.Alternating Current: Peak and RMS value of alternating current/voltage,

reactance, impedance, LCR series circuit, resonance, power in AC circuits, wattless current, AC generator, and transformer.

Unit XV

: Electromagnetic WavesEM Waves: Displacement current, characteristics of electromagnetic waves, transverse nature of EM waves,

electromagnetic spectrum (radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible, ultraviolet, X-rays, gamma rays), and their uses.

Unit XVI

: OpticsRay Optics: Reflection of light, spherical mirrors, mirror formula, refraction of light, total internal reflection,

optical fibers, refraction at spherical surfaces, lenses, thin lens formula, lensmaker's formula, magnification, power of a lens,

combination of thin lenses, refraction and dispersion of light through a prism, scattering of light, and optical instruments

(microscopes, astronomical telescopes).Wave Optics: Wavefront, Huygens' principle, reflection/refraction of plane waves using wavefronts,

proof of laws of reflection/refraction, interference, Young's double slit experiment, expression for fringe width, coherent sources,

diffraction due to a single slit, and polarization.

Unit XVII

: Dual Nature of Matter and RadiationQuantum Theory: Photoelectric effect, Hertz and Lenard's observations, Einstein's

photoelectric equation, particle nature of light, matter waves, de Broglie relation, and Davisson-Germer experiment.

Unit XVIII

: Atoms and NucleiAtoms: Alpha-particle scattering experiment, Rutherford's model of atom, Bohr model, energy levels,

and hydrogen spectrum.Nuclei: Composition and size of nucleus, atomic masses, isotopes, isobars, isotones, radioactivity

(alpha, beta, gamma particles/rays and their properties), radioactive decay law, mass-energy relation, mass defect,

binding energy per nucleon, nuclear fission, and nuclear fusion.

Unit XIX

: Electronic DevicesSemiconductor Electronics: Energy bands in solids, conductors, insulators, intrinsic and extrinsic

semiconductors, p-n junction, p-n junction diode (I-V characteristics), diode as a rectifier, I-V characteristics of LED, photodiode,

solar cell, Zener diode, and basic logic gates (OR, AND, NOT, NAND, NOR).

Unit XX

: Experimental SkillsPractical Familiarity: Vernier calipers, screw gauge, simple pendulum, meter bridge,

resistance of a galvanometer by half-deflection, focal length of mirrors/lenses, refractive index of a glass slab/prism,

and characteristic curves of p-n junction/Zener diodes.
