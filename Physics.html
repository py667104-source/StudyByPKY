Physics Important Units

Class 11 Detailed Syllabus

Unit I

: Physical World and MeasurementUnits and Measurements: SI units, fundamental and derived units, accuracy, precision, errors, significant figures, dimensions of physical quantities, and dimensional analysis.

Unit II

; KinematicsMotion in a Straight Line: Frame of reference, position-time graphs, speed, velocity, uniform/non-uniform motion, and average speed.Motion in a Plane: Vectors, scalar products, vector resolution, relative velocity, projectile motion, and uniform circular motion.

Unit III

: Laws of MotionForce and Inertia: Newton’s laws of motion, momentum, impulse, law of conservation of linear momentum, equilibrium of concurrent forces, static and kinetic friction, laws of friction, rolling friction, and lubrication.Dynamics of Uniform Circular Motion: Centripetal force and examples (vehicles on a level/banked circular road).

Unit IV

: Work, Energy, and PowerWork & Kinetic Energy: Kinetic and potential energy, work-energy theorem, power, conservative and non-conservative forces, conservation of mechanical energy, and elastic/inelastic collisions.

Unit V

: Motion of System of Particles and Rigid BodyRotational Motion: Center of mass of a two-particle system, momentum conservation, moment of a force, torque, angular momentum, conservation of angular momentum, equilibrium of rigid bodies, moment of inertia, radius of gyration, and parallel/perpendicular axes theorems.

Unit VI

: GravitationGravitational Forces: Kepler’s laws of planetary motion, universal law of gravitation, acceleration due to gravity (g) and its variation with altitude/depth, gravitational potential energy, escape velocity, and orbital velocity of a satellite.

Unit VII

: Properties of Bulk MatterMechanical Properties of Solids: Elastic behavior, stress-strain relationship, Hooke's law, Young's modulus, bulk modulus, and shear modulus.Mechanical Properties of Fluids: Pressure due to a fluid column, Pascal's law, buoyancy, viscosity, Stokes' law, terminal velocity, streamline/turbulent flow, critical velocity, Bernoulli's theorem, surface energy, and surface tension (angle of contact, drops, bubbles).Thermal Properties of Matter: Heat, temperature, thermal expansion, specific heat capacity, calorimetry, latent heat, heat transfer (conduction, convection, radiation), thermal conductivity, and Wien’s displacement law.

Unit VIII

: ThermodynamicsThermal Laws & Engines: Thermal equilibrium, zeroth law, first law (internal energy, work, heat), isothermal and adiabatic processes, second law of thermodynamics, reversible/irreversible processes, heat engines, and refrigerators.

Unit IX

: Kinetic Theory of GasesGas Dynamics: Equation of state of a perfect gas, work done on compressing a gas, kinetic theory of gases (assumptions, concept of pressure), kinetic energy and temperature, degrees of freedom, law of equipartition of energy, and mean free path.

Unit X

Unit X

: Oscillations and WavesOscillations: Periodic motion, Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM), oscillations of a spring, kinetic and potential energy in SHM, and a simple pendulum.Waves: Wave motion, longitudinal and transverse waves, speed of wave motion, displacement relation for a progressive wave, principle of superposition of waves, reflection of waves, standing waves in strings/organ pipes, fundamental mode, harmonics, and beats.
