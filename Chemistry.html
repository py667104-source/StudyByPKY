Chemistry Units

PHYSICAL CHEMISTRY

Unit 1: Some Basic Concepts of ChemistryMatter & Laws: Dalton's atomic theory, laws of chemical combination.Core Concepts: Atomic, molecular, and equivalent masses.The Mole: Mole concept, molar mass, percentage composition, empirical and molecular formulae.Stoichiometry: Limiting reagent calculations, balancing chemical equations, concentration terms (Molarity, Molality, Normality, Mole fraction).

Unit 2: Atomic StructureEarly Models: Limitations of Bohr’s model, dual nature of matter (de Broglie), Heisenberg uncertainty principle.Quantum Mechanics: Quantum numbers, orbital shapes (s, p, d).Configurations: Aufbau principle, Pauli’s exclusion principle, Hund’s rule, extra stability of half-filled and completely filled orbitals.

Unit 3: Chemical ThermodynamicsFundamentals: System and surroundings, state/path functions, extensive/intensive properties.First Law: Internal energy (U), enthalpy (H), heat capacity, Hess’s law of constant heat summation, enthalpies of bond dissociation, combustion, and neutralization.Second Law: Entropy (S), Gibbs energy change (Δ G), spontaneity conditions, equilibrium constant connection.

Unit 4: EquilibriumChemical Equilibrium: Dynamic equilibrium, law of mass action, Le Chatelier’s principle (effects of concentration, temperature, pressure).Ionic Equilibrium: Acids, bases, salts (Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, Lewis theories), pH scale, ionization of weak acids/bases, buffer solutions, solubility product (\(K_{sp}\)), common ion effect.

Unit 5: Redox Reactions & ElectrochemistryRedox Core: Oxidation numbers, balancing redox equations (ion-electron and oxidation state methods).Conducted Solutions: Electrolytic conductance, specific and molar conductivity, Kohlrausch’s law.Cells: Electrochemical cells, EMF, Nernst equation and its application to concentration cells.Laws & Storage: Faraday’s laws of electrolysis, dry cells, lead accumulators, fuel cells, corrosion.

Unit 6: SolutionsSolubility: Henry’s law, vapour pressure of liquid solutions, Raoult’s Law (ideal and non-ideal solutions, deviations).Colligative Properties: Relative lowering of vapour pressure, elevation of boiling point, depression of freezing point, osmotic pressure.Abnormal Mass: van 't Hoff factor (i) and its relation to degree of dissociation/association

Inorganic Chemistry

Unit 8: Classification of Elements & PeriodicityBasics: Modern periodic law and long form.Trends: Periodic trends in physical and chemical properties: atomic and ionic radii, ionization enthalpy, electron gain enthalpy, valence, electronegativity, anomalous properties of second-row elements.

Unit 9: Chemical Bonding & Molecular StructureValence & Geometry: Ionic bond, covalent bond, Fajans' rules, dipole moment, VSEPR Theory (predicting molecular shapes).Quantum View: Hybridization involving s, p, and d orbitals, shapes of simple molecules.Molecular Orbitals: Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT) (homonuclear diatomic molecules like H₂, He₂, O₂, N₂), bond order, magnetic properties, Hydrogen bonding

Unit 10: p-Block Elements (Groups 13 to 18)Group Trends: Electronic configuration, atomic radii, ionization enthalpy, and oxidation states.Reactivity: General trends in chemical reactivity, anomalous properties of first elements of each group, catenation, allotropy.

Unit 11: d- and f-Block ElementsTransition Elements: General trends in variable oxidation states, ionic radii, colour, catalytic properties, magnetic properties, interstitial compounds, alloy formation.Compounds: Preparation and properties of KMnO₄ and K₂Cr₂O₇.Inner Transition: Lanthanoid contraction and its consequences, electronic configuration of actinoids.

Unit 12: Coordination CompoundsNomenclature: Introduction, ligands, coordination number, IUPAC nomenclature.Isomerism: Structural and stereoisomerism (geometrical and optical).Theories: Werner’s coordination theory, Valence Bond Theory (VBT), Crystal Field Theory (CFT) (splitting of d-orbitals in octahedral and tetrahedral fields, magnetic moments).

Organic Chemistry

unit13: Purification & Characterization of Organic CompoundsPurification: Crystallization, sublimation, distillation, differential extraction, chromatography (paper and TLC).Qualitative: Detection of nitrogen, sulphur, phosphorus, and halogens (Lassaigne's test).Quantitative: Estimation of carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen (Dumas and Kjeldahl methods), halogens, sulphur, and phosphorus.

Unit 14: Some Basic Principles of Organic Chemistry (GOC)Classification: IUPAC nomenclature of aliphatic and aromatic compounds.Electronic Effects: Inductive effect, electromeric effect, resonance (mesomeric effect), hyperconjugation.Fission: Homolytic and heterolytic bond cleavage, stability of carbocations, carbanions, and free radicals, electrophiles and nucleophiles.

Unit 15: HydrocarbonsAlkanes: Isomerism, conformation of ethane, halogenation mechanism.Alkenes: Geometrical isomerism (cis/trans), Markovnikov’s and Anti-Markovnikov’s addition, ozonolysis, mechanism of electrophilic addition.Alkynes: Acidic character, addition of hydrogen, halogens, water, and hydrogen halides.Aromatic: Hückel’s rule of aromaticity, mechanism of electrophilic substitution (nitration, sulphonation, halogenation, Friedel-Crafts alkylation and acylation).

Unit 16: Organic Compounds Containing HalogensMechanisms: \(S_{N}1\) and \(S_{N}2\) mechanisms, stereochemical aspects (inversion, racemization).Reactivity: Elimination reactions (Saytzeff rule), nucleophilic substitution vs. elimination, reactions of haloarenes (electrophilic substitution, DDT environmental effects).

Unit 17: Organic Compounds Containing OxygenAlcohols: Dehydration mechanism, distinction between primary, secondary, and tertiary alcohols (Lucas test).Phenols: Acidic nature, Reimer-Tiemann reaction, Kolbe’s reaction, electrophilic substitution.Ethers: Preparation by Williamson synthesis, cleavage of ethers by HI.Aldehydes & Ketones: Nucleophilic addition reactions, Aldol condensation, Cannizzaro reaction, Clemmensen and Wolff-Kishner reductions, distinction tests (Tollens', Fehling's).Carboxylic Acids: Acidic strength and factors affecting it, decarboxylation using soda lime.

Unit 18: Organic Compounds Containing NitrogenAmines: Structure, basic strength order in gaseous and aqueous phases, Gabriel phthalimide synthesis, Hoffmann bromamide degradation.Diazonium Salts: Preparation, chemical reactions, coupling reactions, importance in synthetic organic chemistry.

Unit 19: BiomoleculesCarbohydrates: Classification, aldoses and ketoses, structures of glucose and fructose, reducing vs. non-reducing sugars, oligosaccharides (sucrose, lactose, maltose), polysaccharides (starch, cellulose).Proteins: α-amino acids, peptide bond, primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structures of proteins, denaturation.Nucleic Acids: Chemical composition of DNA and RNA, double-helical structure of DNA.

Unit 20: Principles Related to Practical ChemistryQualitative Analysis: Identification of functional groups (unsaturation, alcoholic, phenolic, carbonyl, carboxylic, amino).Inorganic Salt Analysis: Detection of anions (CO₃²⁻, S²⁻, SO₄²⁻, NO₃⁻, Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) and cations (Pb²⁺, Cu²⁺, Al³⁺, Fe³⁺, Zn²⁺, Mn²⁺, Cu²⁺, Ca²⁺, Ba²⁺, Mg²⁺, NH₄⁺).Titrations: Oxalic acid vs KMnO₄, Mohr’s salt vs KMnO₄.
